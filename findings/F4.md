Session Hijacking & JWT Revocation Bypass

## What I Found
A stolen JWT session token (`A_TOKEN`) can be used to fully takeover a user's account. Even after the victim clicks "Logout," the stolen token remains active.

## The Exploit Chain
1. **Session Hijacking:** An attacker obtains a valid `A_TOKEN` of a victim (via XSS, physical access, or WiFi sniffing).
2. **Account Takeover:** The attacker injects the stolen token into their own browser and instantly becomes the victim.
3. **No Logout Protection:** The victim clicks "Logout" and changes their password. The attacker still has full access for the full 20-day token expiry.

## The Bug
The backend does **not** blacklist or revoke tokens when a user logs out or changes their password.

## Impact
- Full account takeover that cannot be stopped by the victim.
- 20-day permanent backdoor access for any stolen token.

## Proof
1. Copied a paid user's `A_TOKEN`.
2. Injected it into a free account's browser cookies and Free account became the paid user.
3. Logged out of the *real* paid account.
4. Again tried same. The free account again got full access after a hard refresh.

## Fix
The backend must immediately invalidate tokens server-side when the user logs out (add the token to a Redis blacklist).
